The history of bitcoin

Despite being around since 2009, until 2013 few people had heard of the cryptocurrency bitcoin. It lived in relative obscurity, talked about mostly within online forums and used chiefly for purchases on the deep web.

Yet five years after its launch, bitcoin started to take off. The cryptocurrency started the year trading at around $13.50 per bitcoin, before rallying to over $1,000 by the end of November caused by new bitcoin exchanges and miners in China entering the marketplace. The price fluctuated somewhat after that, falling to lows of $315 and highs of $1,079.

Then, in the fall of 2017, bitcoin began to experience a meteoric rise which saw it break through the $5,000 mark, before doubling in November to $10,000. In December, the value of a single bitcoin was at $20,000, its highest ever.

Since this peak, bitcoin has continued to be extremely volatile, seeing a number of surges and falls. However, a number of experts believe that bitcoin is still a long way off its full price potential, meaning there is plenty of reason for investment.

Found of the multi-million dollar virus protection firm, John McAfee, argues that due to bitcoin’s scarcity, it has the potential to hit values of more than $1 million. American venture capitalist Tim Draper also considers bitcoin to be a wise investment, stating that a prediction of a 25-fold increase in value over the next few years would be an understatement.

Bitcoin continues to be the most valuable and popular cryptocurrency in the world, with many who hold even .1 of a bitcoin likely to earn a significant amount over time.
